Job Applications

Applying for jobs in computer science is very competitive and can seem daunting at first, but there are ways to push your application over the top and stand out. For example, writing about personal projects you have taken on due to passion for the subject rather than an assingment will show employers you are invested in technology and will have a good attitude to the job. It's also important to look over the job description of what you are applying for, to make sure it is something you would like to do. If it is, you can make links to why you would be good for the job role during your application.

Hiring Process

Understanding the hiring process for jobs in computer science is crucial. This allows you to be as prepared as possible and have the ability to deal with any uncertainties employers ask you about. As briefly mentioned in the application process section, employers look for key qualities in their employees. There will usually be written aspects and interviews during the applicationn - the written part is typically for employers to filter out who has the right qualifications for the job and the right motivation, and interviews allow them to see how you can think on the spot and interact with a team. This is why fully understanding the job description is also important so that you can predict aspects of the hiring process more.

Improvement in quality of life and our planet

Some contrubtions computing has made to quality of life and the planet's state involves the implementation of smart cities, green public transport, sustainable energy sources, assistive technology and more. Our reliance on this tech highlights how important it is that people passionate about computer science join STEM jobs, and also how it is important to encourage underrepresented minorities to get involved in computer science.
